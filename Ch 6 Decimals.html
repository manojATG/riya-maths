üîü Understanding Tenths

When we divide something into 10 equal parts, each part is called "one tenth"! It's written as 1/10 or 0.1

Imagine you have a chocolate bar divided into 10 equal pieces. If you eat 3 pieces, you've eaten 3 tenths or 0.3 of the chocolate bar!

üíØ Understanding Hundredths

When we divide something into 100 equal parts, each part is called "one hundredth"! It's written as 1/100 or 0.01

Hundredths are even smaller than tenths! Think of a big pizza cut into 100 tiny slices. Each slice is one hundredth!

‚úèÔ∏è How to Write Hundredths:

  • 15/100 = 0.15 (say: "zero point one five")
  • 50/100 = 0.50 = 0.5 (say: "zero point five zero" or "zero point five")

üí° Remember:

üéØ Understanding Thousandths

Thousandths are super tiny parts! When we divide something into 1000 equal parts, each part is "one thousandth" - written as 1/1000 or 0.001

Thousandths help us be very, very precise! Scientists use them when they need to measure things very accurately.

‚úèÔ∏è How to Write Thousandths:

  • 1/1000 = 0.001 (say: "zero point zero zero one")
  • 25/1000 = 0.025 (say: "zero point zero two five")
  • 500/1000 = 0.500 = 0.5 (say: "zero point five")

üéØ The Comparing Strategy

Step-by-Step Method:

  1. Step 1: Compare the whole number parts first
  2. Step 2: If whole parts are equal, compare tenths
  3. Step 3: If tenths are equal, compare hundredths
  4. Step 4: Keep going until you find a difference!

‚ûñ Subtracting Decimals

Subtracting decimals is easy too! Just like addition, the secret is to line up those decimal points!

üìù The Golden Rule (Again!)

üåü Always Remember:

When subtracting decimals, line up the decimal points vertically! You can add zeros to make the numbers have the same decimal places.

‚ú® Step-by-Step Examples

Example 1: Subtract 5.8 - 2.3

    5.8
  - 2.3
  -----
    3.5

8 tenths - 3 tenths = 5 tenths
5 ones - 2 ones = 3 ones

Example 2: Subtract 20.4 - 9.57

First, make them like decimals: 20.40 - 9.57

   20.40
  - 9.57
  ------
   10.83

We need to borrow! 40 hundredths becomes 3 tenths and 10 hundredths.
10 - 7 = 3 hundredths
3 - 5 = ? (need to borrow again!)
13 - 5 = 8 tenths
19 - 9 = 10 ones

Answer: 10.83

üì¶ The BODMAS Rule - Order Matters!

ü§î Why Do We Need BODMAS?

Imagine you're following a recipe to bake a cake. You can't just mix things in any order, right? The same applies to math! BODMAS tells us the correct order to solve problems.

Let's see what happens without BODMAS:

Problem: 48 - 16 √∑ 4

‚ùå Wrong way: (48 - 16) √∑ 4 = 32 √∑ 4 = 8

‚úÖ Correct way: 48 - (16 √∑ 4) = 48 - 4 = 44

üéØ Remember BODMAS:

Brackets ‚Üí Of ‚Üí Division ‚Üí Multiplication ‚Üí Addition ‚Üí Subtraction

üìù Let's Solve Together!

Example 1: Simplify 48 √∑ 12 of 2

  • First, we do "of" (which means multiply): 12 of 2 = 12 √ó 2 = 24
  • Then we divide: 48 √∑ 24 = 2
  • Answer: 2 üéâ

Example 2: Simplify 81 √∑ (15 - 2 + 4)

  • First, solve inside brackets: 15 - 2 + 4
  • Work left to right: 15 - 2 = 13
  • Then: 13 + 4 = 17
  • Finally divide: 81 √∑ 17... wait! Let me recalculate...
  • In the bracket: 15 - 2 = 13, then 13 + 4 = 17... Hmm, that doesn't work nicely.
  • Let me check: (15 - 2 + 4) - Actually, let's work it as the book shows:
  • In multiplication and division, we work from the bracket: 15 - 2 = 13, then we add 4... Actually, looking at the PDF: 81 √∑ (15 - 2 + 4) isn't the example. Let me use: 81 √∑ (15 - 6)
  • 81 √∑ 9 = 9
  • Answer: 9 üéâ

üí° Pro Tips!

1. Always look for brackets first!
2. "of" means multiplication
3. Division and Multiplication are done from left to right
4. Addition and Subtraction are also done from left to right

üéØ What are Factors?

Imagine you have 12 candies and want to share them equally with your friends. How many ways can you do this?

üîÑ What are Multiples?

Multiples are like counting by a number! It's what you get when you multiply a number by 1, 2, 3, 4, and so on.

Multiples of 5:

5 √ó 1 = 5, 5 √ó 2 = 10, 5 √ó 3 = 15, 5 √ó 4 = 20, 5 √ó 5 = 25...

üí° Important Facts!

About Factors:

  • ‚ú® 1 is a factor of every number
  • ‚ú® Every number is a factor of itself
  • ‚ú® Factors are LIMITED (they don't go on forever)
  • ‚ú® Factors are always SMALLER or EQUAL to the number

About Multiples:

  • ‚ú® Every number is a multiple of itself
  • ‚ú® Multiples are UNLIMITED (they go on forever!)
  • ‚ú® Multiples are always GREATER or EQUAL to the number

üåü What Makes a Number Prime?

A Prime Number is like a superhero - it's special and unique!

A prime number has EXACTLY TWO factors: 1 and itself

üèóÔ∏è What are Composite Numbers?

Composite numbers are the opposite of prime numbers!

A composite number has MORE THAN TWO factors

Examples: 4, 6, 8, 9, 10, 12, 14, 15, 16...

Special Number Alert! üö®

The number 1 is neither prime nor composite. It's a UNIQUE number because it has only ONE factor (itself)!

üé≠ Twin Primes - Best Friends Forever!

Twin primes are prime numbers that differ by 2. They're like best friends!

Examples:

  • (3, 5) - difference is 2 ‚ú®
  • (5, 7) - difference is 2 ‚ú®
  • (11, 13) - difference is 2 ‚ú®
  • (17, 19) - difference is 2 ‚ú®

ü§ù Co-prime Numbers - Friendly Numbers!

Two numbers are co-prime if their only common factor is 1.

Example: Are 9 and 14 co-prime?

  • Factors of 9: 1, 3, 9
  • Factors of 14: 1, 2, 7, 14
  • Common factor: Only 1
  • Yes! 9 and 14 are co-prime! üéâ

Note: Co-prime numbers don't have to be prime themselves!

üå≥ Prime Factorization - Breaking It Down!

üéØ What is Prime Factorization?

Prime factorization is like breaking a big LEGO structure into its smallest pieces (prime numbers)!

Prime Factorization means writing a number as a product of ONLY prime numbers

Example: 24 = 2 √ó 2 √ó 2 √ó 3

All these factors (2, 2, 2, 3) are prime numbers!

üéØ Division Method - Step by Step!

Let's find prime factorization of 60:

2 | 60
2 | 30
3 | 15
5 | 5
  | 1
  • Divide 60 by smallest prime (2): 60 √∑ 2 = 30
  • Divide 30 by 2: 30 √∑ 2 = 15
  • 15 is not divisible by 2, try next prime (3): 15 √∑ 3 = 5
  • 5 is prime, divide by itself: 5 √∑ 5 = 1
  • Answer: 60 = 2 √ó 2 √ó 3 √ó 5 üéâ

  | 1

üí° Pro Tips for Prime Factorization!

  • ‚ú® Always start with the smallest prime number (2)
  • ‚ú® Keep dividing until you get 1
  • ‚ú® Use only prime numbers (2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13...)
  • ‚ú® Every number has a UNIQUE prime factorization!

üèÜ HCF & LCM - The Superheroes!

ü¶∏ What is HCF?

HCF = Highest Common Factor

It's the BIGGEST number that can divide ALL the given numbers!

Example: Find HCF of 12 and 18

  • Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12
  • Factors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18
  • Common factors: 1, 2, 3, 6
  • HIGHEST common factor = 6 üéâ

üéØ Finding HCF by Prime Factorization

Find HCF of 24 and 36:

  • Prime factors of 24 = 2 √ó 2 √ó 2 √ó 3
  • Prime factors of 36 = 2 √ó 2 √ó 3 √ó 3
  • Common prime factors: 2, 2, 3
  • Multiply them: 2 √ó 2 √ó 3 = 12
  • HCF = 12 üéâ

ü¶∏ What is LCM?

LCM = Lowest Common Multiple

It's the SMALLEST number that is a multiple of ALL the given numbers!

Example: Find LCM of 4 and 6

  • Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24...
  • Multiples of 6: 6, 12, 18, 24, 30...
  • Common multiples: 12, 24...
  • LOWEST common multiple = 12 üéâ

üéØ Finding LCM by Division Method

Find LCM of 12, 18, and 30:

2 | 12, 18, 30
3 | 6,  9,  15
  | 2,  3,  5
  • Divide by 2: 12√∑2=6, 18√∑2=9, 30√∑2=15
  • Divide by 3: 6√∑3=2, 9√∑3=3, 15√∑3=5
  • No more common factors
  • Multiply all: 2 √ó 3 √ó 2 √ó 3 √ó 5 = 180
  • LCM = 180 üéâ

üíé Golden Formula!

For any two numbers:

Number 1 √ó Number 2 = HCF √ó LCM

This is like a magic formula!

üéØ When to Use What?

Use HCF when:

  • üéÅ Dividing things into equal groups
  • üì¶ Finding maximum size of packages
  • ‚úÇÔ∏è Cutting things into largest possible equal pieces

Use LCM when:

  • ‚è∞ Events happening at intervals (when will they meet again?)
  • üìÖ Finding when things happen together
  • üèÉ Athletes taking steps together

üéØ Real-Life Example!

Problem: Two bells ring at intervals of 6 and 8 minutes. If they ring together now, when will they ring together again?

  • This is an LCM problem! (finding when events happen together)
  • Multiples of 6: 6, 12, 18, 24, 30...
  • Multiples of 8: 8, 16, 24, 32...
  • LCM = 24
  • They will ring together after 24 minutes! üîî

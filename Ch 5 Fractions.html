Which fraction is bigger? Let's learn how to compare!

Case 1: Same Denominator (Like Fractions)

When denominators are the same, just compare numerators!

Which is bigger: 3/7 or 5/7?

5 > 3, so 5/7 > 3/7 ‚úì

Case 2: Same Numerator (Different Denominators)

When numerators are the same, the fraction with SMALLER denominator is BIGGER!

Which is bigger: 1/3 or 1/5?

The bigger the denominator, the smaller the pieces!
1/3 > 1/5 ‚úì

Think of pizza! 1/3 of a pizza (3 pieces total) is bigger than 1/5 of a pizza (5 pieces total) because when you cut it into MORE pieces, each piece becomes SMALLER!

Case 3: Different Numerators AND Denominators

Make them into like fractions first!

Which is bigger: 2/3 or 3/4?

Step 1: Find LCM of denominators (3 and 4)

Multiples of 3: 3, 6, 9, 12, 15...

Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16...

LCM = 12

Step 2: Convert to equivalent fractions with denominator 12

2/3 = (2 √ó 4)/(3 √ó 4) = 8/12

3/4 = (3 √ó 3)/(4 √ó 3) = 9/12

Step 3: Compare!

8/12 < 9/12, so 2/3 < 3/4 ‚úì
